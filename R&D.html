Issues with Existing English Learning Resources

Institutions

(Schools, Colleges, English Classes)

Traditional institutions often focus on reading and writing skills. While students may excel in written exams, they often struggle to develop the ability to speak English fluently.

Online Resources

(Apps, YouTube, Websites)

While many online resources are available, they often lack personalized guidance and monitoring. Most apps cater to intermediate or advanced learners, leaving beginners without effective learning tools.

Challenges with Existing Methods and Tricks

Traditional Tricks

(Reading Newspapers, Watching Movies, Expanding Vocabulary)

While helpful for improving vocabulary and comprehension, these techniques are not suitable for beginners who need a foundation in speaking and listening skills.

Learning Methods

(Grammar Translation, Natural Approach, Immersion)

Each method has its limitations. For example, the grammar-translation method focuses too much on rules, while the natural and immersion approaches require environments that may not be easily accessible to all learners.
